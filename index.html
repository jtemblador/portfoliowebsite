About Me

I'm a Computer Science student at California State University Dominguez Hills, graduating in May 2026 and looking for full-time roles in software and ML engineering. My current project is OrbitWatch, a real-time satellite orbit tracker and collision predictor that I'm building from scratch. I wrote a C++ SGP4 orbital propagation engine, bound it into Python via pybind11, built a full coordinate transform pipeline using NASA's SPICE toolkit, and connected everything to a FastAPI backend serving a live Cesium.js 3D globe. Space and satellites have always been a passion of mine, and getting to track real objects orbiting Earth in real time is the most rewarding thing I've built so far.

I got into CS after working in infrastructure, healthcare, and retail. Those experiences taught me how to manage complexity and communicate clearly, which turns out to matter just as much in software as anywhere else.

Outside of code, I lead the worship band at House of the Lord where I play bass. I'm also usually at the gym, stargazing, or building something new.
